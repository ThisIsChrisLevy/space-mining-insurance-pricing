Space Mining Insurance Pricing
Actuarial Risk Modeling for Interstellar Mining Operations

Actuarial modeling and risk analysis for interstellar mining insurance using frequency–severity models, Monte Carlo simulation, and capital risk analysis.

Project Overview

This project was developed for the Society of Actuaries (SOA) 2026 Student Research Case Study Challenge: Actuaries in Space – The Pricing Frontier.

The objective of the project is to design and price insurance products for Cosmic Quarry Mining Corporation (CQMC), a fictional company operating large-scale mining missions across multiple solar systems.

Because space mining involves extreme operational uncertainty, the analysis applies actuarial modeling techniques to estimate potential losses and determine appropriate insurance pricing and capital requirements.

The project proposes an integrated insurance portfolio covering four major risk areas:

Equipment Failure Insurance

Cargo Loss Insurance

Workers’ Compensation Insurance

Business Interruption Insurance

The modeling framework combines frequency–severity loss models, Monte Carlo simulation, and scenario stress testing to estimate expected losses and evaluate extreme tail-risk events.

Project Objectives

The primary goals of this project are to:

Design insurance products for emerging space mining operations

Model expected claim frequencies and severities

Estimate aggregate loss distributions across coverage lines

Evaluate catastrophic scenarios such as solar storms and orbital debris collisions

Determine appropriate premium structures and capital reserves

Provide a comprehensive actuarial risk assessment for interstellar mining operations

Methodology

The actuarial analysis follows a frequency–severity modeling framework.

Frequency Modeling

Loss events are modeled using probability distributions derived from historical data in related industries, including:

Satellite insurance markets

Aerospace launch reliability statistics

Terrestrial mining safety data

Severity Modeling

Loss severities reflect the estimated economic value of:

Spacecraft infrastructure

Robotic mining systems

Transported cargo

Operational revenue streams

Monte Carlo Simulation

To capture uncertainty in extreme environments, the model simulates thousands of operational years to generate:

Aggregate loss distributions

Expected loss estimates

Tail risk scenarios (P99 events)

Stress Testing

Extreme risk scenarios were evaluated, including:

Major orbital debris collisions

Solar radiation storms

Launch vehicle failures

System-wide communication disruptions

These events represent low-frequency but high-severity risks common in space operations.

Key Risk Factors Analyzed

The study examines risk exposures across three solar systems.

Solar System	Primary Hazard
Helionis Cluster	High solar radiation exposure
Bayesia System	Dense orbital debris fields
Oryn Delta	Long transport routes

These environmental differences were incorporated into the pricing framework through risk load adjustments.
